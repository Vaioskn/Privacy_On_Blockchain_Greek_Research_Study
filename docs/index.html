Privacy On Blockchain Greek Research Study

The paper is an in-depth exploration of the intersection between blockchain technology and privacy. It begins with an overview of blockchain technology, emphasizing its reliance on Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT) and Peer-to-Peer (P2P) networks. It explores the use of private and public keys, a critical aspect of user identity and transaction security, then delves into cryptographic methods that add to and improve privacy in blockchain systems (zero-knowledge proofs, ring signatures, Stealth addresses etc). The concept of mixing is also mentioned, a method used to obscure the traceability of transactions. The paper then provides a comprehensive comparison of different blockchain privacy systems and categorizes them into Public, Private, Hybrid and Consortium. The "Analysis-Synthesis" section of the paper provides an analysis of the problems surrounding blockchain, as well as solutions to these problems, through the techniques and approaches already presented up to this point. It is written in Greek in its entirety.
